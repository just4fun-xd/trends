How to read this chart

Everything starts at 100 — that is normalization, not a bug. All three lines are rescaled so that their first value equals 100. This puts dollars-per-barrel, index points and strategy P&L on one comparable scale: a value of 117 simply means +17% cumulative growth since the first date.

Price (grey, right axis) — growth of $100 invested in the asset itself (buy & hold). This is the benchmark: if a strategy line is below the grey line over a stretch, the strategy underperformed simply holding the asset there (usually with far less risk — compare how smooth the lines are, not only how high).

Equity curves (left axis) — growth of $100 given to each strategy. Flat shelves = the strategy is out of the market (P&L frozen); sloped segments = in a position. The two y-axes have different ranges, so compare shapes across axes, not absolute heights.

Bottom panel — position size over time. Height is the applied position after vol-targeting, so it can exceed 1.0 (e.g. 1.08 = 108% notional because volatility was low) or stay below it. Zero = out of the market. This panel explains the shelves above: equity only moves while a position bar is non-zero.

Controls: mouse wheel = zoom around cursor, drag = pan, ◀ ▶ = step left/right, Reset = full range. Both panels always show the same time window.
